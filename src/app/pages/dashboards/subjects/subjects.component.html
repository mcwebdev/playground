In programming, particularly with libraries such as RxJS, Subjects are a special type of Observable that can multicast to many Observers. Here are some types of Subjects and their characteristics:

Subject:

Technical:

A type of Observable that can multicast to many Observers. Values are sent to all subscribers equally, with no replay of old values.

Analogy:

Pure live TV, if late you missed it.

BehaviorSubject:

Technical:

Similar to a Subject, but it stores the latest value emitted to its consumers, and whenever a new Observer subscribes, it will immediately receive the "current value" from the BehaviorSubject.

Analogy:

Your 'currently watching list', always on the latest episode.

ReplaySubject:

Technical:

Similar to a BehaviorSubject but it can also record a part of the Observable execution and replay it to new subscribers.

Analogy:

Replaying episodes missed.

AsyncSubject:

Technical:

It only emits the last value of the Observable execution (and only the last value) and only if the Observable completes.

Analogy:

All about the season finale reveal.
